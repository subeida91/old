Microsoft Research Internship

Role: Researcher


I collaborated with project partners in Microsoft Research to investigate how intelligibility impacts the use of a computer vision system used by blind people. The system was designed to improve finding people in social situations. I designed the study ensuring all materials were accessible to blind people, that the study was ethical and would tightly control any extraneous variables while being conducted in a naturalistic setting, and operationalised how to measure “intelligibility”. The results from the 13 participants were analysed using both qualitative and quantitative methods. Our work was published in March 2020 by IUI. After the publication we were invited to present our work at their conference.


IUI 2020 presentation: Investigating the intelligibility of a computer vision system for blind users

Takeaways: During the process learnt how to design a study that was inclusive and also how to scope a project and distil its findings into a publication. Designing an inclusive user study pushed me to think about ways to make all design more accessible for Blind users and those with other disabilities. Through my work, I learnt how to design with a broader and more deliberate sense of empathy and impact.
